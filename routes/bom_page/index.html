Bill of Materials

The following are all of the parts you need to build your car. We've included the approximate costs of each and where you can find them. We'll continue to update this page.

You can cut down on time and money by ordering a partial kit from the DonkeyCar store. It's run by the original founders of donkey car and profits are used to fund development.

It is worth noting the design of the parts are slightly improved over the standard build as it uses better parts that are only available in large quantities or are harder to get. The Donkey Store builds are open source like all others.

Desert Monster seems to be the most available right now.

The Desert Monster, Short Course Truck and Blaze all require adapters which can be easily printed or purchased from the donkey store.

PCA9685 16 Channel 12 Bit PWM Servo Driver for Arduino and Raspberry Pi

~$12.00

In-order to make the motors spin on the car, a very specific pulse is required to tell the motors exactly what to do.  

This method is called Pulse-Width Modulation (PWM) and uses small variations in voltage to affect the speed or signal being sent to the motors.  

This controller (PCA9685) enables us to send these pulses to the motor.

This will do all the processing of images, sending signals to the control board and run the web-portal for controlling the car.
